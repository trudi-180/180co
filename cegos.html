Breakfast Talk Ideas
1
The Accidental Manager: Why Great Performers Make Struggling Leaders
Most organisations promote on performance, not people-leadership readiness, then act surprised when strong performers struggle to lead. This session unpacks the four shifts every new manager must make and what separates those who make the leap from those who stay stuck. Participants leave with a self-diagnostic, a first-90-days reference, and one concrete behaviour to try next.
60–90 mins · New managers, HR, senior leaders
2
From Bottleneck to Multiplier: Lead Without Being the Answer to Everything
For leaders who've become the person everyone routes around — the one whose calendar is full because nothing moves without them. This session reframes being indispensable as a leadership failure, not a strength, and introduces a quick decision-delegation test for whether something should go to you, your team, or be handled by policy. Using a short worksheet to map their own recurring requests, participants identify at least two or three decisions or tasks they can hand off this week, and leave with language ready to use the next time someone tries to escalate something back to them.
60–90 mins · Senior managers, directors, founders

Breakfast Talk Ideas (continued)
3
Stop Having the Same Conversation Twice: Feedback That Actually Changes Behaviour
Most feedback fails not because people don't care, but because of how it's delivered — vague, late, or buried in praise so it doesn't land. This highly interactive session introduces a situation–behaviour–impact–ask structure and has participants practise it live in pairs, drafting and rehearsing one piece of real, overdue feedback they need to deliver. Everyone leaves with a printable quick-reference card and, more importantly, an actual conversation prepped and ready to have within the week — not just a framework.
75–90 mins · All managers · Works as L&L
4
AI Won't Take Your Job — But a Leader Who Uses AI Might
A grounded, non-hype look at what AI actually changes for people leadership — not coding or automation hype, but what it means for how managers coach, communicate, and develop their teams when AI can do more of the "easy" parts of the job. The session maps where AI genuinely helps and where it can't replace a leader's judgment on things like feedback, coaching, and culture, surfacing low-risk ways to start using it as a thinking partner. Participants leave with reduced anxiety about AI replacing their leadership role and one specific AI-assisted habit to try in the next two weeks — using it to prep for a hard conversation, for instance, not to have it for them.
60–90 mins · All levels · High demand in 2026
